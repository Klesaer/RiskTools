# Kamarura Risk Manager (KRM)

The Kamarura Risk Manager (KRM) is intended to provide a structured framework for monitoring, analysing, and governing risk information across pricing, balance sheet, and regulatory processes. In practice, KRM acts as the control and coordination layer that connects data, models, workflows, and reporting outputs.

## Purpose

KRM supports the consistent execution of risk management activities by:

- capturing and validating source data,
- applying agreed methodologies and assumptions,
- producing risk metrics and management information,
- supporting governance, controls, and escalation.

## Core responsibilities

The KRM framework typically covers the following areas:

- data intake and quality controls,
- calculation and aggregation of risk metrics,
- scenario analysis and sensitivity reporting,
- exception management and governance workflows,
- integration with downstream reporting and decision support.

## Operating model

A robust KRM operating model normally includes clear ownership of inputs, calculation logic, reporting outputs, and review controls. Documentation should therefore describe:

- the governance structure,
- the data lineage and control points,
- the periodic reporting cadence,
- the escalation and approval steps for material findings.
