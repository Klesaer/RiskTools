# IRRBB

Interest rate risk in the banking book (IRRBB) refers to the risk that changes in interest rates will adversely affect the economic value of a bank's assets and liabilities, as well as its future earnings and balance sheet structure. It is a core prudential and balance sheet risk topic and is closely linked to profitability, liquidity, and capital management.

## Overview

IRRBB arises from structural mismatches between the repricing characteristics, maturity profiles, and behavioural assumptions of assets, liabilities, and off-balance-sheet positions. The risk may manifest through:

- changes in net interest income,
- movements in economic value,
- sensitivity to basis and curve shifts,
- changes in customer behaviour, such as prepayment or deposit withdrawal patterns.

## Core concepts

A formal IRRBB framework typically considers:

- the identification of material rate-sensitive positions,
- the measurement of sensitivity to parallel and non-parallel yield curve shifts,
- the analysis of economic value and earnings impact,
- the assessment of management actions and hedging effectiveness.

## Governance and management

IRRBB is normally monitored through a combination of risk metrics, scenario analysis, limit frameworks, and reporting to senior management and governance committees. The effectiveness of the framework depends on sound data quality, clear ownership, and a consistent set of assumptions.
